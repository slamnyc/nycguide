Transportation

New York City is best explored by foot - it is the best way to familiarize yourself with the many neighborhoods that make the city so unique. Subways, buses, and cabs are also available if walking isn't the most convenient option!

Subways & Buses

Purchase a MetroCard to gain access to the trains and buses that travel all across the city. Each trip is $2.75 and options such as the week unlimited ($30) or month unlimited ($116.50) are available as well. Check out the MTA website to plan ahead.

Taxis

Taxis are available 24/7 if you prefer traveling via car over the subway or by foot. When hailing a cab, look for cabs whose numbers are illuminated which indicates that they are available. The minimum metered fare is $2.50, and an additional $1 surcharge is added to the meter Monday-Friday, 4-8pm, and a 50-cent surcharge is added at night, 8pm-6am.. Cabs will accept cash or credit.
