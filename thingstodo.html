Things to Do


Metropolitan Museum of Art
Visit the largest art museum in the US, containing thousands of collections from around the world.
Grand Central Terminal
The busiest train station in the country, come and shop or take a tour!
Central Park Biking
Central Park is a best explored on a bicycle, offering views from flat, hilly and scenic terrain.
Ice Skating
Enjoy a nice wintry thrill in the city gliding on ice in Bryant Park, the Rockefeller Center, or Central Park.

9/11 Memorial & Museum
A tribute of remembrance and honor to the nearly 3,000 people killed in the terror attacks of September 11, 2001.
The High Line
A public park built on a historic freight rail line above the streets on Manhattan's West Side.
Brooklyn Bridge
Walk across one of the oldest suspension bridges in the country connecting the boroughs of Manhattan and Brooklyn.
Chinatown
Explore the cultural pocket of Chinatown and enjoy local treats and shopping.

Shopping
Block out time to check out some of NYC's department stores and countless of boutiques. The options are endless!
Tenement Museum
Take a walk through and learn more about America's urban immigration history.
Ellis Island Museum
Learn about Ellis Island's history before, during, and after its use as America's immigration station.
Yankee's Stadium
Take a trip up to the Bronx and check out the Yankee's stadium.
